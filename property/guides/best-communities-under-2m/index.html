Best areas to buy property in Dubai under AED 2 million

Updated June 2026 · 4 min read

AED 2 million doesn't go as far in Downtown or Palm Jumeirah, but it opens up a wide range of communities where you can buy a solid apartment or even a townhouse — while also crossing the threshold for Golden Visa eligibility.

Jumeirah Village Circle (JVC)

JVC is the most searched community in this budget. Studios and one-bedrooms regularly trade well under AED 1M, with two-bedroom apartments comfortably inside the AED 2M range. The trade-off is a still-maturing retail and dining scene, though that's changing fast with new openings every quarter.

Dubai South

Dubai South sits near Al Maktoum International Airport and Expo City, and is one of the more affordable entry points for both apartments and townhouses. It's a longer commute from Downtown or the Marina, so it suits people who work locally or don't mind the drive. Long-term upside is significant given the airport expansion plans.

Dubailand

Dubailand covers a huge stretch of inland Dubai and includes a mix of villa communities and apartment clusters at accessible price points, with good value on space per square foot compared to coastal areas. Communities like Villanova and Arabian Ranches 3 sit within this corridor.

Al Furjan

Al Furjan offers townhouses and villas that can land inside or just above AED 2M, with easier access to Sheikh Zayed Road and a more established community feel than some newer developments. Good schools nearby make it popular with families.

Jebel Ali

Jebel Ali Village and the surrounding area offers some of the most competitive pricing in Dubai for townhouses and villas, with a quieter, more suburban feel. It suits buyers prioritising space over proximity to the city centre.

Town Square (Nshama)

Popular with young families — three- and four-bedroom townhouses can fall within or near this budget, with parks and community retail built in from day one. One of the better-planned affordable communities in Dubai.

The honest trade-off

Across all of these communities: you're giving up proximity to the coast and the most established retail in exchange for more space and a lower entry price. If your budget is firm at AED 2M, the right area depends on whether you prioritise commute time, rental yield, or long-term capital appreciation.
