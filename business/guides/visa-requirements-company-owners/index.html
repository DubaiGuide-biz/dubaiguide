Get my match → The full sequence 1 Establishment Card Your company needs an Establishment Card before any visas can be processed. Your free zone or setup provider handles this as part of the formation process — it typically takes 2–5 days. 2 Entry permit or status change If you're outside the UAE, you'll receive an entry permit to enter the country. If you're already in the UAE on another visa, you'll go through an in-country status change instead. Status changes are straightforward but require you to temporarily exit and re-enter if you're on a visit visa. 3 Medical test A standard medical fitness test — blood test and chest X-ray — is mandatory for all visa applicants. This is done at a MOHAP-approved clinic and typically takes 1–2 days for results. 4 Emirates ID registration You'll need to visit an ICA service centre to register biometrics for your Emirates ID. The card is typically issued within 1–2 weeks of registration. 5 Visa stamp The final step places the residence visa in your passport. Visas are typically valid for 2–3 years depending on the license type and free zone, and are renewable. Timeline End to end, from Establishment Card to a stamped visa, typically takes two to four weeks if documents are in order. The medical test and Emirates ID appointment are usually the bottleneck rather than the paperwork itself. Bringing your family Sponsoring a spouse and children requires showing a minimum income — this varies by emirate and authority, but is generally in the range of AED 4,000–10,000 per month depending on the sponsorship category and number of dependents. Your setup provider can confirm the current requirement for your specific situation. Multiple shareholders Each shareholder needs their own visa processed through the same sequence. Factor both the cost and the timeline into your planning if you're setting up with partners — two visas take roughly the same time as one, but the medical and ID steps need to be scheduled individually.
